📧 Email & Outreach
Template 01

Cold Email Outreach

Use when: Reaching out to potential clients, partners, or investors for the first time.

Write a cold outreach email for the following scenario: My name / company: [YOUR NAME / COMPANY] Target recipient: [JOB TITLE] at [TYPE OF COMPANY] What I offer: [YOUR PRODUCT OR SERVICE — 1 sentence] The specific problem I solve for them: [PAIN POINT] One social proof element: [TESTIMONIAL / RESULT / CLIENT NAME] Desired CTA: [e.g. "15-minute call" / "reply with interest"] Requirements: - Subject line: curiosity-driven, under 8 words, no clickbait - Opening: reference something specific about their business (I'll fill in the actual detail) - Body: 3-4 sentences max — focus on their problem, not my features - CTA: low-friction, single ask - Tone: confident but not pushy, conversational Output: subject line + email body (under 120 words)
💡 Pro Tip: Run this prompt 3× and pick the best subject line. A/B test subject lines — they drive 80% of open rates.
Template 02

Follow-Up Email Sequence (3-Part)

Use when: A prospect hasn't replied to your first outreach after 3–5 days.

Write a 3-email follow-up sequence for a prospect who hasn't replied to my cold email. Context: - My original email was about: [BRIEF SUMMARY OF FIRST EMAIL] - My offer: [PRODUCT/SERVICE] - The prospect's role: [JOB TITLE / COMPANY TYPE] Follow-up rules: - Email 2 (Day 4): Add new value — share a quick tip or insight relevant to them, soft re-mention of the offer - Email 3 (Day 8): Social proof angle — a brief client result or case study, renewed CTA - Email 4 (Day 14): "Break-up" email — graceful, leaves door open, zero pressure Format each email with: - Subject line - Body (under 80 words) - Tone: human, not salesy
💡 Pro Tip: Email 4 ("break-up") often gets the highest reply rate. People respond when they feel the window is closing.
Template 03

Testimonial Request Email

Use when: You've delivered results for a client and want a written testimonial or case study quote.

Write a short email asking a satisfied client for a testimonial. Context: - Client name: [FIRST NAME] - What we did together: [PROJECT / SERVICE] - Key result delivered: [SPECIFIC OUTCOME — e.g. "30% increase in leads"] - How I'll use it: [Website / LinkedIn / sales materials] Requirements: - Make it extremely easy for them — offer a 2-sentence template they can edit - Suggest 3 specific things they could mention (results, experience, recommendation) - Include a direct link placeholder: [TESTIMONIAL FORM LINK] - Subject line: personal, not corporate - Under 100 words total Also write the suggested 2-sentence template I can provide them as a starting point.
💡 Pro Tip: Always give them the starting draft. Clients want to help but rarely have time to start from scratch. Pre-written = 5× more responses.
Template 04

Onboarding Email Sequence (3-Part)

Use when: A new customer just purchased — you need to welcome them and set them up for success.

Write a 3-email onboarding sequence for a new customer. Product/service: [YOUR PRODUCT OR SERVICE] Customer goal: [WHAT THEY WANT TO ACHIEVE] Key first action they should take: [STEP 1] Main feature or module to highlight in week 1: [FEATURE] Support channel: [EMAIL / CHAT / COMMUNITY LINK] Email structure: - Email 1 (Immediate): Welcome, confirm purchase, single next step, warm tone - Email 2 (Day 2): Quick win — show them one result they can get TODAY - Email 3 (Day 5): Check-in, anticipate the #1 question or objection, offer help Tone: friendly, expert, genuinely helpful Each email: under 150 words with a clear subject line and CTA
💡 Pro Tip: The Day 2 "quick win" email reduces refund rates dramatically. Get them to a small win fast and they're emotionally invested.
Template 05

Weekly Newsletter Template

Use when: Sending your regular email newsletter to subscribers.

Write a weekly newsletter edition using this structure: Newsletter name / niche: [NAME] — focused on [TOPIC/INDUSTRY] This week's main theme or hook: [1-sentence angle] My audience: [WHO THEY ARE + their biggest pain/goal] Sections to write: 1. HOOK (2-3 sentences): provocative question or surprising stat related to the theme 2. MAIN INSIGHT (150-200 words): one valuable insight, framework, or story — no fluff 3. THIS WEEK'S TIP (50 words): single actionable thing they can do today 4. TOOL OR RESOURCE SPOTLIGHT (50 words): recommend one tool, article, or resource with why 5. CALL TO ACTION (30 words): one ask — reply, click, or book Tone: [FORMAL / CONVERSATIONAL / WITTY] Subject line: write 3 options (curiosity, benefit, and question styles) Preview text: 1 line that teases the main insight
💡 Pro Tip: The "reply to this email" CTA trains your audience to engage, which improves inbox delivery rates over time.
💰 Sales & Marketing
Template 06

Sales Page Copy Generator

Use when: Building or rewriting a product/service landing page.

Write a high-converting sales page for my offer. Product/Service name: [NAME] Price: [€/£/$PRICE] Target customer: [DESCRIBE YOUR IDEAL BUYER IN 2 SENTENCES] Top 3 pain points it solves: 1. [PAIN 1] 2. [PAIN 2] 3. [PAIN 3] Key deliverables / what they get: [LIST THE MAIN COMPONENTS] Transformation: Before buying they feel [STATE BEFORE]. After, they feel [STATE AFTER] Social proof available: [TESTIMONIALS / RESULTS / LOGOS] Guarantee: [MONEY-BACK / RESULTS / NONE] Primary CTA button text: [BUY NOW / GET ACCESS / etc.] Write the full page using this structure: 1. Headline + subheadline (benefit-focused) 2. Problem section (agitate the pain) 3. Solution introduction (your offer as the bridge) 4. What's included (bullet list, benefit-framed) 5. Social proof section (placeholder for testimonials) 6. Objection-busting FAQ (5 questions) 7. Guarantee section 8. Final CTA with urgency
💡 Pro Tip: The best headlines sell the transformation, not the product. "Go from [problem] to [result] in [timeframe]" outperforms clever every time.
Template 07

Objection Handler Script

Use when: Preparing for sales calls or writing objection-handling FAQ copy.

Generate objection-handling scripts for my sales conversations. My offer: [PRODUCT / SERVICE / PRICE] My target customer: [DESCRIPTION] Write confident, empathetic responses to these 6 common objections: 1. "It's too expensive." 2. "I need to think about it." 3. "I don't have time right now." 4. "Can you send me more information?" 5. "I need to talk to my partner/boss first." 6. [YOUR CUSTOM OBJECTION: add your most common one here] For each objection, provide: - ACKNOWLEDGE: validate their concern (1 sentence) - REFRAME: shift the perspective (1-2 sentences) - QUESTION: a clarifying question to uncover the real objection - CLOSE ATTEMPT: a soft close after handling (1 sentence) Tone: consultative, not pushy. Never argue. Always lead with curiosity.
💡 Pro Tip: "I need to think about it" almost always means "I'm not convinced yet." The reframe question reveals what's really holding them back.
Template 08

Proposal / Pitch Deck Outline

Use when: Pitching a project to a client or creating a business proposal.

Create a professional proposal outline for the following: Client/prospect: [COMPANY NAME / TYPE] Project or service: [WHAT YOU'RE PROPOSING] Key pain points they have: [2-3 PROBLEMS YOU IDENTIFIED] Your proposed solution: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION] Timeline: [HOW LONG IT TAKES] Investment: [PRICE / PRICING STRUCTURE] Your differentiator: [WHY YOU OVER COMPETITORS] Create a 7-section proposal structure: 1. Executive Summary (problem + solution in 2 sentences) 2. Understanding Your Situation (show you get their pain) 3. Our Approach (how you'll solve it, step by step) 4. What's Included (scope — be specific) 5. Timeline & Milestones (key dates) 6. Investment (pricing, what's included, payment terms) 7. Next Steps (clear action for them to take) Also write the opening paragraph for section 1 and section 2.
💡 Pro Tip: Section 2 ("Understanding Your Situation") wins proposals. Clients hire people who understand their problem — not just those with the best solution.
📱 Content & Social Media
Template 09

Social Media Content Calendar (1 Week)

Use when: Planning a week of social content across platforms.

Create a 7-day social media content calendar for my business. Business type: [YOUR BUSINESS] Target audience: [WHO FOLLOWS YOU] Goal this week: [BRAND AWARENESS / LEAD GENERATION / SALES / ENGAGEMENT] Platforms: [e.g. LinkedIn + Instagram + Twitter/X] Content pillars (pick 3-4 that define your brand): - [PILLAR 1: e.g. "Education/Tips"] - [PILLAR 2: e.g. "Behind the scenes"] - [PILLAR 3: e.g. "Social proof"] - [PILLAR 4: e.g. "Promotion/Offer"] For each of the 7 days, provide: - Platform - Content pillar - Post hook (first line — must stop the scroll) - Post body (100-150 words) - CTA - Hashtag suggestions (5) - Content format: [image / video / carousel / text] Distribute content pillars evenly. Max 1 promotional post per week.
💡 Pro Tip: The hook is 80% of performance. Write 5 hook options for each post and pick the most provocative one. Test, don't guess.
Template 10

Blog Post Outline Creator

Use when: Writing SEO blog posts or thought leadership articles.

Create a detailed outline for a blog post / article. Topic: [YOUR TOPIC] Target keyword (primary): [MAIN SEO KEYWORD] Secondary keywords: [2-3 RELATED TERMS] Target reader: [WHO IS READING THIS] Their goal in reading: [WHAT DO THEY WANT TO LEARN/SOLVE] Desired length: [800 / 1200 / 2000+ words] Tone: [PROFESSIONAL / CONVERSATIONAL / AUTHORITATIVE] Provide: 1. SEO-optimised title (include keyword, under 60 chars) — 3 options 2. Meta description (150 chars, include keyword and CTA) 3. Introduction outline (hook + context + what they'll learn) 4. 5-7 H2 subheadings with brief notes on what to cover in each 5. 3 internal link placeholder spots [LINK TO: topic] 6. Conclusion outline (summary + next step CTA) 7. Featured snippet opportunity: write one paragraph optimised for Google's featured snippet
💡 Pro Tip: The featured snippet paragraph (answer the question in 40-60 words, starting with "X is...") can 3× your organic clicks from that keyword.

Product Description Writer

Use when: Writing product listings for e-commerce, SaaS, or service pages.

Write a compelling product description for my product/service. Product name: [NAME] Category: [TYPE OF PRODUCT] Price: [PRICE] Top 3 features: 1. [FEATURE 1] 2. [FEATURE 2] 3. [FEATURE 3] Top 3 benefits (what each feature means for the buyer): 1. [BENEFIT 1] 2. [BENEFIT 2] 3. [BENEFIT 3] Target buyer: [WHO IS THIS FOR?] Use case: [WHEN/HOW DO THEY USE THIS?] Brand tone: [PLAYFUL / PROFESSIONAL / LUXURY / TECHNICAL] Write: 1. Short description (50 words): for above-the-fold product pages 2. Long description (200 words): SEO-rich, benefit-led, includes features 3. Bullet points (5): scannable highlights for product listings 4. Tagline (under 10 words): memorable, benefit-focused
💡 Pro Tip: Lead with the benefit, not the feature. "Sleep through the night" beats "Advanced noise cancellation technology" every time.
Template 12

FAQ Section Generator

Use when: Adding FAQ sections to sales pages, product pages, or support docs.

Generate a comprehensive FAQ section for my product/service. Product/service: [NAME AND BRIEF DESCRIPTION] Price: [PRICE] Target customer: [WHO BUYS THIS] Common concerns (if you know them): [LIST ANY YOU'VE HEARD] Delivery/format: [HOW IS IT DELIVERED? DIGITAL / PHYSICAL / SERVICE] Refund policy: [YOUR POLICY] Generate 10 FAQ items covering: - What it is / who it's for - How it works / what happens after purchase - Results / what to expect (realistic) - Time investment required - Refund / guarantee policy - Technical requirements (if any) - Support / help available - Why this over alternatives - Pricing / payment options - Next steps to get started Format: Question in bold, Answer in 2-4 sentences. Answers should reduce anxiety and reinforce value.
💡 Pro Tip: FAQs are objection handlers in disguise. For every FAQ, ask: "What fear does this answer?" and write toward eliminating that fear.
Template 13

Customer Service Response Templates

Use when: Handling common customer service scenarios quickly and professionally.

Write customer service email templates for these 6 common scenarios: My business: [TYPE OF BUSINESS] My product/service: [WHAT YOU SELL] Brand tone: [FORMAL / FRIENDLY / EMPATHETIC] Write response templates for: 1. REFUND REQUEST (empathetic, clear next steps, preserve relationship) 2. COMPLAINT ABOUT PRODUCT/SERVICE (acknowledge, apologise, resolve, retain) 3. BILLING ISSUE / WRONG CHARGE (clear, factual, reassuring, fast resolution) 4. "WHERE IS MY ORDER/ACCESS?" (calm, helpful, set expectations) 5. FEATURE REQUEST (thank them, set honest expectations, no false promises) 6. CANCELLATION REQUEST (don't guilt, understand why, offer alternative if genuine, process cleanly) For each: subject line + 4-6 sentence response Placeholders: use [CUSTOMER NAME], [ORDER NUMBER], [RESOLUTION DATE] etc. Rule: every response ends with a clear next step.
💡 Pro Tip: For complaints, the word "I'm sorry this happened" (not "sorry for the inconvenience") tests measurably higher for customer satisfaction.

Template 14

LinkedIn Profile Optimiser

Use when: Rewriting your LinkedIn profile to attract clients, investors, or employers.

Rewrite my LinkedIn profile sections to attract [TARGET: clients / employers / investors]. My name: [NAME] Current role/title: [TITLE] What I actually do (plain English): [DESCRIPTION] Who I help: [TARGET AUDIENCE] The results I create for them: [OUTCOMES/RESULTS] My background (key roles, years): [BRIEF HISTORY] Credentials / notable achievements: [AWARDS, CLIENTS, METRICS] What I want people to do after viewing my profile: [CTA] Rewrite: 1. HEADLINE (under 220 chars): keyword-rich, outcome-focused, not just job title 2. ABOUT SECTION (300 words): story format — hook, problem you solve, how, proof, CTA 3. EXPERIENCE SECTION (for current role): 5 bullet points, achievement-framed with metrics 4. SKILLS (suggest 10 strategic skills to list) 5. FEATURED SECTION hook: one-line teaser for a pinned post or article
💡 Pro Tip: Your headline appears in search results and connection requests. It's your most-read LinkedIn copy. Make it about the reader's outcome, not your job title.
Template 15

Meeting Agenda + Summary Generator

Use when: Running or documenting client meetings, team calls, or stakeholder check-ins.

I need two things: a pre-meeting agenda and a post-meeting summary template. Meeting type: [CLIENT CALL / TEAM STANDUP / STRATEGY SESSION / SALES CALL] Duration: [30 / 60 / 90 minutes] Attendees: [ROLES — e.g. "client CEO, client marketing lead, my account manager"] Goal of this meeting: [WHAT NEEDS TO BE DECIDED OR ACCOMPLISHED] Topics to cover: 1. [TOPIC 1] 2. [TOPIC 2] 3. [TOPIC 3] PART 1 — PRE-MEETING AGENDA: Create a timed agenda with clear objectives for each section. Include: welcome/intro, each topic, decision points, action item capture, close. PART 2 — POST-MEETING SUMMARY TEMPLATE: Create a template I fill in after the meeting with: - Decisions made - Action items (owner, deadline, task) - Open questions / parking lot - Next meeting: date, agenda items Tone: professional but concise. Designed to be sent within 1 hour of meeting close.
💡 Pro Tip: Sending the summary within 1 hour of a meeting doubles follow-through on action items. Speed signals professionalism.
